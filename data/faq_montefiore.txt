Question:  For what reasons will the Medical Center grant a leave of absence under FMLA?
Answer:  The Medical Center will grant a leave of absence under FMLA for the following reasons:

1. **Birth of a son or daughter and to care for the newborn child**.
2. **Placement of a child with the Associate through adoption or foster care and to care for the child after placement**.
3. **To care for a covered family member (spouse, child, parent, or qualified domestic partners) with a serious health condition**.
4. **To care for the employee’s own serious health condition which makes the employee unable to perform the essential functions of their position**.
5. **Qualifying Exigency Leave**: For reasons resulting from the spouse, son, daughter, or parent of the Associate being called or ordered to active duty in the Armed Forces in support of a contingency operation. Specific qualifying exigencies include:
   - Short-notice deployment (seven or less calendar days),
   - Military events and related activities,
   - Childcare and school activities,
   - Financial and legal arrangements (e.g., powers of attorney, military identification cards, wills or trusts),
   - Counseling arising from active duty or call to active duty status,
   - Rest and recuperation (up to five days of leave to spend time with a covered military member on temporary leave),
   - Post-deployment activities, and
   - Other agreed-upon similar events.
6. **Military Caregiver Leave**: To care for a spouse, son, daughter, parent, or next of kin who has incurred a serious illness or injury in the line of duty in the Armed Forces, including the National Guard and Reserves, and is rendered medically unfit to perform their military duties.

Question:  How is the 12-month period for FMLA leave calculated?
Answer:  The 12-month period for FMLA leave is calculated using a “rolling” method measured backward from the time the leave is commenced. This means that as of the day that leave is commenced, an eligible Associate is entitled to 12 weeks of leave, less any other FMLA leave taken by the Associate in the preceding 12 months.

Question:  Does an Associate get 12 weeks of leave for each serious health condition under FMLA?
Answer:  No, an Associate does not get 12 weeks of leave for each serious health condition under FMLA. Associates get a total of 12 weeks of leave in a rolling 12-month period, regardless of how many medical certifications have been approved. This means if the Associate or their covered family members have multiple serious health conditions that qualify under the FMLA, the Associate does not get 12 weeks for each condition but rather a total of 12 weeks.
