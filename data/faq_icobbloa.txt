Q: What is the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)?
A: The Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) is an entitlement that provides eligible employees of covered employers with up to 12 workweeks of leave in a 12-month rolling calendar year, or up to 26 workweeks of leave during a single 12-month rolling calendar year to care for a covered service member. This leave is unpaid, job-protected, and allows for the continuation of benefits as if the employee had not taken leave. The leave can be taken for specified family and medical reasons.

Reference: Definitions

Q: How many workweeks of leave can an eligible employee take under FMLA within a 12-month period?
A: An eligible employee can take up to 12 workweeks of leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) within a 12-month period. Additionally, in certain situations related to a covered family member's service in the Armed Forces, eligible employees may qualify for up to 26 workweeks of leave.

Q: What must employees provide to initiate or continue a leave of absence?
A: Employees must provide timely and appropriate verification to initiate or continue a leave of absence. This includes completing a Leave of Absence Request Form, stating the reasons for the absence, and providing documentation supporting the request. For leaves related to a serious health condition of the employee or an immediate family member, a health provider's certification of the need for leave is required. Q: What is the general policy on reinstatement after an approved leave of absence?
A: The general policy on reinstatement after an approved leave of absence is as follows:

1. **Leaves of Six (6) Months or Less:**
   - Upon expiration of the leave of absence, if an employee reports to work and is able to perform the essential functions of his/her original position, the employee shall be reinstated to the original position or a comparable position with equivalent pay.

2. **Leaves Exceeding Six (6) Months:**
   - If the leave of absence exceeds six (6) months, upon expiration of the leave of absence, if the employee is able to perform the essential functions of his/her original position, reinstatement is at the discretion of the Department Head/Agency Head/Elected Official. If the employee has accrued leave available when the leave of absence ends, the employee shall be entitled to utilize such leave.

3. **For Health-Related Leaves:**
   - The Department Head/Agency Head/Elected Official may require health care certification of the employee's fitness to return to work and/or may require a fitness for duty evaluation by the County physician before permitting the employee to return to work.

4. **Notification Prior to Return:**
   - An employee who has been on a leave of absence for a period of 30 calendar days or longer must notify the Department Head/Agency Head/Elected Official of his/her intention to return at least 10 calendar days prior to returning from leave. Failure to file such notice or to report promptly at the expiration of the leave of absence, except for satisfactory reasons submitted in advance, may be cause for dismissal.

A: How long can an employee take a leave of absence for personal reasons?
